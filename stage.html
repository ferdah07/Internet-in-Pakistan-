The History of Internet in Pakistan

In 1994, Digi-com Pakistan introduced internet services for the first time using the SCPC satellite backbone link with a speed of 64 Kbps. This was made possible with the help of an engineer named Nasser Khan Ghazi, who became the first Pakistani to use online browsing.

After this, the growth of the internet accelerated, especially with the introduction of mobile services. Pak-tel launched mobile services using first-generation (1G) technology. Internet access further improved as more companies, such as Mobilink, entered the market with second-generation (2G) mobile technology.

In 2001, the first broadband, DSL, was launched by Micro-net Broadband. With advancements in mobile services, the market expanded significantly. By 2005, many companies upgraded their infrastructure to 2.5G, enabling internet connectivity through General Packet Radio Service (GPRS) on mobile phones. In 2006, Transworld Associates launched the first submarine cable linking Karachi to the UAE. Following this development, several companies, including Wateen Telecom, Nayatel, and Cybernet, launched their broadband services.
